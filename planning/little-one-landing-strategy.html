01 The Big Idea

One world ties all three ideas together: a charming vintage airfield on a bright, lemon-yellow day. Everything on the page belongs at that airfield, so the themes never feel stapled together — they live in the same place.

Three things make the blend click:

1. "Landing" already means two things

A plane comes in for a landing; a baby is landing too. The domain name does the heavy lifting before a single word of copy. The whole site is the arrivals board for one tiny, long-awaited flight.

2. Lemon-yellow is also classic aviation

The most beloved little plane in the world — the Piper J-3 Cub — is famously lemon yellow. So the citrus palette and the airfield palette are the same palette. That's exactly why a "light citrus accent" works: the sunny yellow is already doing the lemon's job in every section, so actual lemons only need to appear once.

3. "Squeeze you" is what you do when someone lands

When a flight lands, you throw your arms open and squeeze. So "we can't wait to squeeze you" bridges the plane (landing) and the lemon (squeeze) through the most human thing in the world — hugging the baby. It earns its place as the emotional headline.

4. Finn isn't a guest — he works here

Detection dogs are airport security. Making Finn the airfield's Head of K-9 Security doesn't fight the theme; he's a native of it. He gets one dedicated, scene-stealing section and a quiet "Secured by Finn" badge in the footer.

03 Naming & Voice

The trick to a themed site is a consistent translation layer: every ordinary baby-shower element gets an airfield name. Keep it warm and light — a friendly gate-agent voice, never a pun overload.

Translation glossary

OrdinaryOn the airfield
RSVP→Check In / Confirm Your Seat
Event details→Your Boarding Pass
Schedule→Today's Flight Plan
Registry→Pack the Cargo Hold / Care Package
Directions→Flight Path & Ground Control
Hosts→Ground Crew / Air Traffic Control
Guests→Passengers
Dress code→Flight Attire
RSVP deadline→Boarding Closes
Refreshments→In-Flight Refreshments
Security / house rules→Finn, Head of K-9 Security

Voice samples

"Ladies and gentlemen, please fasten your seatbelts — a very important little passenger is inbound."Hero / announcer voice
"This is your final boarding call. Seats are filling up — check in below."RSVP nudge
"All passengers will be screened for treats. Belly rubs are mandatory."Finn, Security

Rule of thumb: one playful line per section, then plain and clear for the actual logistics. The theme should never make a guest hunt for the address or the time.

04 Site Structure

A single scrolling page with a slim sticky nav. A dotted "flight path" line threads down the page with a little plane following it, connecting each stop. Order:

Hero — Cleared for Landing

anchor: #top

The emotional opener. Big tagline "We can't wait to squeeze you," the baby's arrival line, date + airfield, and a primary Check In button. Yellow prop plane, soft clouds, sunny sky.

Your Boarding Pass — the what / when / where

anchor: #details

The single most important block, styled as a tear-off boarding pass: Passenger, Flight (Baby Shower), Date, Time, Gate (address), Flight Attire, Boarding Closes. Crystal clear, theme second.

Today's Flight Plan — the itinerary

anchor: #plan

A light timeline along the flight path: Boarding (arrive & mingle) → Taxi (nibbles) → Take-Off (games) → Cruising Altitude (food & cake) → Descent (gifts & gratitude).

Airport Security — Meet Finn

anchor: #security

The dedicated Finn beat. His K-9 ID badge, a goofy bio, and any gentle "house rules" delivered in his voice. Contained to one section so the gag lands and doesn't wear out.

Pack the Cargo Hold — registry

anchor: #registry

"Help us stock the cabin for our littlest passenger." Registry links styled as luggage tags. (Inspo registry was Pottery Barn Kids — swap in yours.)

Fresh-Squeezed at the Airfield — the lemon moment

anchor: #refreshments

The one place real lemons appear. The runway lemonade stand, the signature sip, and a reprise of "we can't wait to squeeze you." This is where the citrus accent pays off.

Check In — RSVP

anchor: #rsvp

"Confirm your seat." Name, number of passengers, dietary notes, a message to the family, and the Boarding Closes date. Simple form.

Flight Path & Ground Control — getting there

anchor: #navigation

Address, map, parking, and a "Ground Crew" contact for questions. Compass rose / runway map motif.

Footer — Cleared by the Tower

anchor: #footer

Hosts, date reprise, event hashtag, and the quiet "Secured by Finn · K-9 Unit" badge.

07 Spotlight — The Lemonade Moment

Fresh-Squeezed at the Airfield

This is the single block where real lemons show up, so the citrus reads as a deliberate grace note rather than a competing theme.

  • Headline: "We can't wait to squeeze you" — reprised here as the payoff.
  • Sub: "Cool off at the runway lemonade stand — fresh-squeezed, all afternoon."
  • Visual: a sunny lemonade-stand / citrus cluster; lemon-blossom accents.
  • Optional: "Squeeze the day" as a tiny tagline on a cup illustration.

If you'd rather, this block can merge into the Flight Plan as the "in-flight refreshment" stop — but giving it its own beat is what makes the inspiration-photo pun sing.

09 Build & Handoff Notes

For the eventual Svelte build (hand this to Claude Code — Cowork stays out of the repo):

  • One route, component per section: Hero, BoardingPass, FlightPlan, Security, Registry, Refreshments, Rsvp, Navigation, Footer — composed in +page.svelte.
  • Content in one place: a single event.js/+page.js data object (names, date, address, registry URLs, RSVP target) so nothing is hard-coded in markup.
  • Design tokens: drop the palette + the three font families into CSS custom properties / app.css (see Section 05).
  • Motion (light): the plane-on-flight-path can be a scroll-linked SVG; respect prefers-reduced-motion.
  • RSVP: decide the mechanism — mailto, Google Form embed, Formspree, or a small Supabase table. Affects whether this stays fully static.
  • Accessibility: the theme is decorative; keep real date/time/address as plain, selectable, screen-reader-friendly text, not baked into images.
